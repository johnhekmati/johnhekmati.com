About

Background

I started in electrical engineering — digital signal processing — where the habit is simple: find what always holds, then pressure-test the weak links.

Later I moved into accounting credentials and enterprise systems work: Dynamics 365 Finance & Supply Chain consulting, with further depth across SAP and Oracle environments. Those rooms reward precise explanations; fuzzy ones fail exams and client work alike.

At home I’m a father of five. Three of our children have complex care plans. That isn’t a pitch — it’s the operating reality that shaped how I design: methods have to survive interruption, incomplete attention, and hard deadlines. If it only works in a quiet four-hour block, it doesn’t work.

The question I optimize for. Can you still do the work a month later — or did you only finish the material?

Path

How I got here

Four chapters. Each raised the cost of unclear structure.

  • 1 · Engineering Signal-processing work: isolate what is solid, then stress what isn’t.
  • 2 · Real constraints Family and care logistics ruled out anything that only works without interruption. Clean restarts became mandatory, not nice-to-have.
  • 3 · Hard domains Accounting and ERP delivery — where vague understanding shows up under exam pressure and in production systems.
  • 4 · Product The Cognition Factory: structured deep learning, honest practice under pressure, and a way to save your place so next session isn’t a cold start.
